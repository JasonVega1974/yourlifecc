# My Walk with God — Pathway Index

**A guided discipleship pathway for YourLife CC**

---

## What this pathway is

"My Walk with God" is a structured journey through the major stations of the Christian life — from first curiosity all the way to a lifelong horizon of Christlikeness. Each station is a self-contained page in the app that tells a person where they are, what this step means, how to take it, and what comes next.

The design principle: **invitation, never a scoreboard.** No one moves to the next station because they passed a test. They move when they're ready, at their own pace, with a real human (pastor, mentor, community) as the primary guide — the app provides structure and daily tools, but never replaces the church.

## What still needs pastor decisions before this pathway ships

The following are `[PASTOR INPUT]` decisions that the content drafts flag but do not resolve. Bring this list to your pastoral review conversation.

### 1. Station order

The current order reflects the spec's recommendation. Your pastor may want to change:

- **Baptism timing** — Station 05 places baptism right after conversion. Some traditions baptize quickly after belief; others have a longer preparation process. The station's position may need to move.
- **The Holy Spirit** — Station 09 (Walking in the Spirit) is placed after the "First Steps" chapter. Some traditions introduce the Holy Spirit at conversion; Pentecostal/charismatic traditions often treat a subsequent "baptism of the Spirit" as a separate event. Your pastor should decide when and how this station appears.
- **Freedom and Wholeness** — Placeholder position is between stations 09 and 10 (Chapter 3). Your pastor may move it.

### 2. Which stations to keep, merge, or cut

This draft includes 13 stations (plus the Freedom placeholder). That may be too many for your context. Your pastor may want to:
- Merge Stations 01 and 02 (Curious + Gospel) for simplicity.
- Merge Stations 03 and 04 (Accepted Christ + Assurance) for a less fragmented feeling.
- Cut or defer Making Disciples (Station 12) for new believers who aren't ready to think about that yet.
- Collapse Discipleship (10) and Becoming Like Christ (13) since they cover related ground.

There is no wrong answer. A pathway of 7–9 stations is easier to navigate and less likely to feel overwhelming to a new believer.

### 3. The teaching content in every "Understand this" section

Every station is marked: *[PASTOR REVIEW — This section carries the most spiritual weight and needs pastoral approval before going live.]* The spiritual teaching in these sections was drafted by an AI content author as a starting framework. It must be reviewed, corrected, and in some cases replaced by your pastor before any of this reaches a real user. This is the most important task in the review process.

Key areas with the most theological weight:
- **Station 02:** The gospel presentation. This is the most consequential section in the entire pathway. Get it exactly right for your tradition.
- **Station 05:** Baptism theology and practice. Mode, timing, meaning — these all differ across traditions and must reflect your church's view.
- **Station 09:** The Holy Spirit. Significant differences across traditions on gifts, the Spirit's work, and what "walking in the Spirit" means practically.
- **Station 13:** Sanctification. Broadly stated in the draft but may need tradition-specific language (e.g., entire sanctification for Wesleyan traditions).

### 4. Human connection at each station

Every station has a "You're not alone" section that points to a real person. Before this ships, you'll want to:
- Confirm the pastoral contact mechanism in the app is live and staffed.
- Decide how your church handles discipleship inquiries coming through the app.
- Consider whether you want to build a "talk to a pastor" flow for specific stations (Accepted Christ, Baptism especially).

### 5. The Freedom and Wholeness station

This station needs pastor plus licensed counselor co-authorship. See `00-FREEDOM-STATION-NOTE.md` for the full rationale. It should not ship in the same phase as the other stations unless it has received that review.
