# Station 12 — Making Disciples

**Chapter 4: Living It Out | Twelfth station on the path**

---

## 1. Where you are

You've been following Jesus long enough that the path behind you is real. And somewhere around you, there is a person who is where you were — curious, or just beginning, or hurting and not knowing where to turn.

You are now further along the path than someone. That means you have something to give.

---

## 2. What this step is

Making disciples means helping someone else begin and grow in their walk with God — not by being a perfect example, but by walking alongside them and sharing what has been real in your own experience.

This is not for people who have it all figured out. It is for people who are willing to say: *here's what I've found, let me walk with you.*

---

## 3. Understand this

> [PASTOR REVIEW — This section carries the most spiritual weight and needs pastoral approval before going live.]

Jesus' last instruction to his followers before his ascension is called the Great Commission. Go, he said, and make disciples of all nations — teaching, baptizing, accompanying them in learning to obey everything he had commanded (Matthew 28:18-20). This was not a suggestion for the pastors in the group. It was given to all of them.

Acts 1:8 adds the shape: starting close, moving outward. You begin with the people nearest you — your household, your neighborhood, your circle — and the ripple expands from there.

Paul described how the chain works: what you've received, give to reliable people who can in turn give it to others (2 Timothy 2:2). Every generation of Christians exists because someone in the previous generation did this.

What does disciple-making actually look like for an ordinary person? It usually looks like:

- **Having honest conversations** about what you believe and why, with people who don't yet believe.
- **Living differently** in a way that makes people curious about the source.
- **Inviting someone** to church, to a Bible study, or to the beginning of a conversation about Jesus.
- **Walking alongside someone** who is earlier in the journey than you are — answering their questions, praying with them, pointing them to Scripture.

You do not need to be an expert. You need to be willing.

## 4. Scripture anchor

**Matthew 28:18-20** — Jesus told his followers: all authority is his — so go, make disciples of all nations, baptize them, teach them to follow his ways. He will be with them. [paraphrased; full passage is longer than 15 words]

**Acts 1:8** — "But you will receive power when the Holy Spirit comes on you; and you will be my witnesses in Jerusalem, and in all Judea and Samaria, and to the ends of the earth." — start close, move outward.

**2 Timothy 2:2** — What you've received, entrust to reliable people who can pass it on to others still. [paraphrased — CHECK exact wording of 2 Timothy 2:2]

---

## 5. Take the step

Think of one person in your life who is spiritually curious, spiritually skeptical, or spiritually early — someone who might be open to a real conversation. Pray for that person by name this week. Then look for one natural opening to have a genuine conversation about faith — not a lecture, not a sales pitch. A real conversation.

---

## 6. How you'll know

- You are actively praying for someone who doesn't yet know Jesus.
- You've had at least one honest conversation about your faith with someone who doesn't share it.
- You're meeting regularly with someone who is earlier in the walk than you are, helping them take their next steps.

---

## 7. Tools for this step

- **Who Is Jesus** — Something you can share with someone who is in the early-curious stage. A starting point for a conversation, not a closing argument.
- **Story Mode** — A narrative overview of the Bible that is accessible to someone who has never read it.
- **Faith Academy lessons** — If you're walking alongside a new believer, the early lessons are a guided curriculum you can work through together.

---

## 8. Reflect

*Think about the person or people who helped you begin your walk with God. What did they do or say that made a difference? What would it mean to do something similar for someone else?*

---

## 9. Pray

*God, show me the person you want me to walk with. I don't have all the answers, and I'm still figuring out my own walk — but I'm willing to share what's been real. Help me to see people the way you see them: not as projects, but as people you love and are pursuing. Use me. Amen.*

---

## 10. You're not alone

Your church likely has structured training for evangelism and discipleship — ask your pastor what's available. If your church has a small group or discipleship program, joining (and eventually leading) is one of the most natural on-ramps for this station. You don't have to figure out how to disciple people from scratch; your church has infrastructure for it.
