# Station 03 — Accepted Christ

**Chapter 1: Coming Home | Third station on the path**

---

## 1. Where you are

Something changed. You've heard the gospel story and something in you said yes — or is saying yes right now. This station marks that moment.

This is the most important step on the entire path. Welcome.

---

## 2. What this step is

"Accepting Christ" is a common phrase for a decision that is both simple and life-altering: turning to Jesus, trusting what he did on the cross, and choosing to follow him. It's not a religious transaction or a membership form. It's a change of direction — the beginning of a relationship, not the end of one.

Some people experience this as a single clear moment. Others can't point to an exact day — they just know they've crossed over from looking to following. Both are real.

---

## 3. Understand this

> [PASTOR REVIEW — This section carries the most spiritual weight and needs pastoral approval before going live.]

Becoming a Christian is not about being good enough. It's about trusting that Jesus already did what you could never do for yourself. The Bible says this is how it works: you acknowledge that Jesus is Lord — that he is who he said he was — and you believe in your heart that God raised him from the dead (Romans 10:9). That trust, when it's genuine, marks the beginning of a new life.

This doesn't mean your life will instantly feel different. It doesn't mean all your questions disappear or your problems go away. What it means is that you are now in a different relationship with God — not as a stranger or an outsider, but as a child (John 1:12). The Bible says that if anyone is in Christ, they are a new creation — something genuinely new has begun (2 Corinthians 5:17).

Some people pray a prayer at this moment to make it concrete. That prayer is not the thing that saves you — the trust is. But praying out loud can help make real what is happening inside.

---

## 4. Scripture anchor

**Romans 10:9** — If you declare with your mouth that Jesus is Lord and believe in your heart that God raised him from the dead, you will be saved. [paraphrased; sense is accurate — CHECK VERSE]

**John 1:12** — "Yet to all who did receive him, to those who believed in his name, he gave the right to become children of God." [14 words — verify ESV/NIV exact text]

**2 Corinthians 5:17** — "Therefore, if anyone is in Christ, the new creation has come: The old has gone, the new is here!" — something genuinely new begins.

---

## 5. Take the step

If you're ready: pray. Use the prayer below as a guide, or say it in your own words — what matters is the honest intention behind it, not getting the words exactly right. When you're done, mark this station as taken.

Then tell someone. A pastor, a parent, a friend who follows Jesus. Saying it out loud to another person makes it more real and opens the door to the support you'll need for the next steps.

---

## 6. How you'll know

- You've prayed (or committed in your heart) to trust Jesus and follow him.
- Something in you feels settled — even if the emotions aren't dramatic.
- You want to know more. There's a new hunger for something you couldn't fully name before.

Not everyone cries. Not everyone feels a wave of emotion. That's okay. Trust is a quiet thing sometimes. What matters is whether you meant it.

## 8. Reflect

*What was it — a person, a moment, a passage, a feeling — that finally moved you from curious to willing? Write it down. You'll want to remember it.*

---

## 9. Pray

This is a model prayer — say it in your own words if you'd like.

*Jesus, I believe you are who you said you are. I believe you died and rose again, and that what you did covers everything I've done wrong and everything I am. I'm turning toward you now. I don't want to keep living on my own terms — I want to live with you. Thank you for not waiting until I had it together. I'm yours. Amen.*

---

## 10. You're not alone

Please tell a pastor or ministry leader about this step as soon as you can — ideally this week. This is not a step to take alone and then quietly move on from. A pastor can pray with you, explain what comes next, and help you take root in a community of people who will walk with you.

If you're connected to YourLife CC's church, the pastoral contact is available in the app. Reach out today.
