# Station 01 — Curious / Come and See

**Chapter 1: Coming Home | First station on the path**

---

## 1. Where you are

You're curious. Maybe you're not sure what you believe, or someone you trust has a faith you don't fully understand yet. Maybe life has pushed you toward questions you've never had to ask before.

You're in exactly the right place. This is the beginning of the walk — and the beginning is always welcome here.

---

## 2. What this step is

This station is for anyone who is looking but hasn't yet decided what they're looking for. You don't need a belief system to be here. You don't need to know how to pray or what a church service looks like or whether the Bible is true.

All you need is an honest question and the willingness to keep going. That's it. The rest unfolds from there.

---

## 3. Understand this

> [PASTOR REVIEW — This section carries the most spiritual weight and needs pastoral approval before going live.]

The Christian story begins with a God who is not hiding. The Old Testament writers say that when people seek God with honest hearts, they find him — not because they're worthy, but because he's not far away (Jeremiah 29:13; Acts 17:27).

When two curious people followed Jesus and asked where he was going, he didn't quiz them or hand them a reading list. He said two words: "Come and see" (John 1:39). That invitation is still open.

Doubt and curiosity are not the opposite of faith. Many of the people in the Bible — Thomas, Gideon, the disciples caught in a storm — asked hard questions. Faith is not certainty; it's choosing to keep walking toward what you're starting to glimpse.

---

## 4. Scripture anchor

**Jeremiah 29:13** — God says through the prophet: you will seek me and find me when you seek with all your heart.

**Psalm 34:8** — "Taste and see that the Lord is good; blessed is the one who takes refuge in him."

**John 1:39** — Jesus' answer to two curious followers who asked where he was going: "Come and you will see."

---

## 5. Take the step

Open the "Who Is Jesus" section in this app and spend 10 minutes with it. Don't try to believe anything yet — just read and let the questions come. Write down one question that surfaces.

---

## 6. How you'll know

You're not being graded here. Some signs that this station is opening something in you:

- Your questions feel less like obstacles and more like doors.
- You find yourself wanting to know more rather than walking away.
- Something you read or heard about Jesus has stayed with you longer than you expected.

None of these are requirements. Curiosity looks different for everyone.

---

## 7. Tools for this step

- **Who Is Jesus** — A good first read for someone exploring the central figure of the Christian faith.
- **Proof & Prophecy** — If you're the type who needs to know whether this holds up historically and intellectually before you go further.
- **Story Mode** — A narrative walk through the Bible that doesn't assume any prior knowledge.

---

## 8. Reflect

*What's one question about God, Jesus, or faith you've always wanted an honest answer to — the kind you might be afraid to ask out loud?*

---

## 9. Pray

This is just a conversation starter — use your own words if these don't fit.

*God, I'm not sure I know how to talk to you, or whether you're even listening. But something in me is curious, and I'm willing to look. If you're real, would you meet me here? Help me not be afraid of the questions. Amen.*

---

## 10. You're not alone

Questions like these are best explored with a real person, not just an app. Consider reaching out to a pastor, a trusted friend who has a faith you respect, or a church that welcomes questions without pressure. This step is the beginning of a conversation that belongs in community.

If you're connected to YourLife CC's church, a pastoral contact is available — tap the contact link in the app.
