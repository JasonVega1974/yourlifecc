# Freedom & Wholeness — Station Reserved for Pastoral and Counselor Authorship

## Why this station does not have draft content

The "Freedom & Wholeness" station is a deliberate blank in this batch of drafts. Every other station in the "My Walk with God" pathway has been drafted as a starting framework for pastoral review. This one has not, and that is on purpose.

## What this station is meant to cover

The Freedom & Wholeness station addresses the interior work of grace — walking toward freedom from patterns, wounds, and habits that keep people stuck. That includes things like addiction, compulsive behaviors, shame from the past, anger, grief, and old wounds that have not yet healed. Done well, it can be one of the most important stations on the entire path. Done carelessly, it can shame the exact people it is trying to help.

## Why it requires a different author

**1. The pastoral dimension.**
This station touches areas where a pastor's knowledge of their congregation, their theology of spiritual warfare and healing, and their understanding of what "freedom" actually means in their tradition all matter deeply. Generic wording risks trivializing real pain or making promises that are not the app's to make.

**2. The counselor dimension.**
Some of what this station addresses — addiction, trauma, compulsive behavior, abuse — overlaps directly with territory that requires a licensed mental health professional. The line between spiritual encouragement and clinical guidance is real, and an app should never be someone's primary support for these struggles. Content here must include clear, prominent direction to professional help and must not imply the app or a digital pathway is sufficient.

**3. The harm potential.**
A row of unlit milestones ("free from porn," "free from anxiety," "free from anger") could function as a public shame board for someone who is still in the middle of the fight. The design and the wording must both be reviewed by people who understand that risk.

## What needs to happen before this station ships

- A pastor (ideally Pastor Tim or your church's discipleship leader) reviews the station scope and writes or substantially rewrites the spiritual teaching.
- A licensed counselor or Christian mental health professional reviews for harm-avoidance and appropriate referral language.
- Any list of "freedom areas" is confirmed to be private, self-chosen, and framed as "areas I want to walk toward freedom in" — never a checklist the app evaluates.
- A specific crisis/counseling referral is included (e.g., a pastoral contact, a counseling resource, or a crisis line where appropriate).
- The "how you'll know" section receives special care — progress in freedom is not linear, and markers must not feel like a test someone is failing.

## Placement in the pathway

Per the product spec, this station sits in Chapter 3 (Growing Deep), between Walking in the Spirit and Discipleship. Its exact position is a `[PASTOR INPUT]` decision. Some traditions may place healing work earlier (before church involvement) or later (as an ongoing dimension of discipleship). The placement should reflect your church's understanding of sanctification and healing.
