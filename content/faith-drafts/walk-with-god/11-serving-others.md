# Station 11 — Serving Others

**Chapter 4: Living It Out | Eleventh station on the path**

---

## 1. Where you are

Welcome to Chapter 4. Everything built in the first three chapters — the gospel, the identity, the prayer life, the Scripture, the community, the depth — now turns outward. Faith that stays inside is not the Christian faith.

This is where the walk starts changing the world around you.

---

## 2. What this step is

Serving others is giving your time, energy, skill, and attention for someone else's benefit — without needing something in return. In the Christian life, this is not something that falls only on pastors or people with a lot of free time. It is something every person who follows Jesus is called to.

The good news: you don't have to save the world today. You just have to notice one person who needs something you can give.

---

## 3. Understand this

> [PASTOR REVIEW — This section carries the most spiritual weight and needs pastoral approval before going live.]

Jesus was not subtle about this. He said that he himself came not to be served but to serve — to give his life as a payment on behalf of many (Mark 10:45). That's not just a one-time historical act. It's a model. The shape of Jesus' life — turned outward, poured out for others — is the shape he invites his followers into.

He took it further: what you do for someone who is hungry, thirsty, a stranger, sick, or in prison — you are doing it to him (Matthew 25:40). Service to people in need is not a category of religious activity separate from knowing Jesus. It is meeting Jesus.

Paul writes that you are called to freedom — but that freedom is not for indulging yourself. It's for serving one another in love (Galatians 5:13). The freedom that comes with being a new creation is partly freedom *from* selfishness — and the natural use of that freedom is to give it away.

Service is also one of the things that keeps you from becoming the kind of Christian who knows a lot but is gradually becoming less like Jesus. Something about hands-on, practical love for another person keeps the theology honest.

---

## 4. Scripture anchor

**Mark 10:45** — Jesus said that even he did not come to be served, but to serve — and to give his life as a ransom for many. [paraphrased; full verse is 27 words]

**Matthew 25:40** — "Truly I tell you, whatever you did for one of the least of these brothers and sisters of mine, you did for me." — service to people in need is a direct encounter with Jesus.

**Galatians 5:13** — Paul writes that the freedom Christians have is not for self-indulgence — it's for serving one another in love. [paraphrased — CHECK Galatians 5:13 exact text]

---

## 5. Take the step

Do one concrete act of service today — for someone in your home, your neighborhood, your church, or your community. It doesn't have to be big. Cook a meal for someone who is overwhelmed. Help a neighbor. Volunteer for one hour at something your church does. The specifics are less important than the doing.

---

## 6. How you'll know

- You have served someone this week — specifically, visibly, practically.
- You are plugged into at least one regular avenue of service in your church or community.
- Service is starting to feel less like a duty and more like something that gives you energy — a sign that you're discovering how you're built to give.

---

## 7. Tools for this step

- **Daily devotional / Verse of the Day** — A daily awareness of God's character — generous, compassionate, present — that shapes the instinct to give.
- **Verse of the Day** — Passages about justice, compassion, and love fuel the motivation to serve.

---

## 8. Reflect

*Who in your life needs something you could give — time, attention, help, presence? What would it cost you to give it? What might it cost them if you don't?*

---

## 9. Pray

*God, open my eyes to the person right in front of me who needs something I have. Help me not to be so caught up in my own path that I miss the moment to serve. Give me the kind of love that is not theory — the kind that gets up and does something. Amen.*

---

## 10. You're not alone

Your church almost certainly has structured serving opportunities — volunteer teams, community care programs, outreach events. Ask your pastor or a ministry leader how to get plugged in. Serving alongside other people from your church is one of the fastest ways to go from being a regular attender to feeling like you actually belong.
