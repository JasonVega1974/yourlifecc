# Station 04 — Assurance and New Identity

**Chapter 1: Coming Home | Fourth station on the path**

---

## 1. Where you are

You've said yes to Jesus. And now — maybe the very next day — the doubts showed up. *Did that really happen? Was it real? Do I feel different enough?*

This station is for that moment. And the answer to those questions matters.

---

## 2. What this step is

Assurance is the settled confidence that what happened between you and God is real — not because of how you feel on a given morning, but because of what God actually did. New identity is understanding who you are now because of that. Together, they're the foundation everything else is built on.

This is not a station you graduate from. These truths are worth returning to whenever the doubts come back — and they will.

---

## 3. Understand this

> [PASTOR REVIEW — This section carries the most spiritual weight and needs pastoral approval before going live.]

Here is something important: your feelings are not the measure of your salvation. The Bible does not say "you shall know you have eternal life because you feel completely at peace every day." It says you can *know* — and the basis of that knowing is not your emotional state but God's word and what Jesus did (1 John 5:13).

The fear that "it didn't really happen" is very common in new believers. It doesn't mean it didn't happen. It usually means you're learning to trust someone you've only just met. That's not failure — that's normal.

Here is what the Bible says is true about you now:

- There is no condemnation — none — for those who are in Christ (Romans 8:1).
- You have been adopted as a child of God, not as a servant or an outsider (Romans 8:15-16).
- You are a new creation — the person you were before this is not who you are now (2 Corinthians 5:17).
- You are known, chosen, and loved — not because you earned it, but because God decided it (Ephesians 1:4-5).

These are not feelings. They are facts. Your job is to let them become things you believe, even on the days you don't feel them.

---

## 4. Scripture anchor

**Romans 8:1** — "Therefore, there is now no condemnation for those who are in Christ Jesus." — Not "less condemnation." None.

**1 John 5:13** — John says he wrote his letter so that people who believe in Jesus can *know* they have eternal life. [paraphrased; full verse is longer than 15 words — CHECK the exact text of 1 John 5:13]

**2 Corinthians 5:17** — "Therefore, if anyone is in Christ, the new creation has come: The old has gone, the new is here!" — this is about identity, not just behavior.

---

## 5. Take the step

Pick one truth from the list in section 3 that you most need right now. Write it down — on your phone, on paper, somewhere you'll see it. Read it every morning this week. Not as a religious exercise, but as a reminder of what is real.

---

## 6. How you'll know

- You find yourself returning to these truths when the doubts surface — and the doubts have less power each time.
- You begin to notice a difference in how you see yourself — less defined by your past, more by who God says you are.
- The idea of being loved by God starts to feel less like a foreign concept and more like something you're slowly beginning to believe.

---

## 7. Tools for this step

- **Scripture memory** — The best long-term tool for this station. Memorizing Romans 8:1 and 2 Corinthians 5:17 is a deposit you'll draw on for years.
- **Faith Academy lessons** — Look for lessons on salvation and identity.
- **Prayer journal** — Write your doubts and then write the truth beside them. The practice is more powerful than it sounds.

---

## 8. Reflect

*Which part of your old identity — who you were before this, how you saw yourself — is hardest to let go of? What would it mean to actually believe you're a new creation?*

---

## 9. Pray

*God, I'm not always sure I feel what I thought I'd feel. But I'm learning that you're not based on how I feel. Your word says there's no condemnation for me now — and I want to believe that. Help me to know it, not just know about it. Teach me who I am now that I'm yours. Amen.*

---

## 10. You're not alone

If doubts about your salvation are persistent or heavy, that is worth talking through with a pastor — not because something is wrong with you, but because this is exactly the kind of conversation pastors are trained for. A mentor or small group can also speak truth into the doubting moments you'll have. Don't try to figure out your identity alone.
